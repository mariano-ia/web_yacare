The decision

You can't tell this story with a static site

What we didn't want

Club websites are built for clubs that already exist: finished history, archives, news. A page that gets published once and sits still.

What we built

A living experience. The site tells our present moment: what's happening today, in real time, with the thrill of watching ourselves come to life.

The concept

It's not a website.
It's an experience that tells our story in real time.

How it feels

Every module is built to spark emotion

The countdown

The days to the first whistle pulse on the home page. The club's clock takes center stage: every time you visit, there's less time left.

The founders wall

A live board where every new founder shows up with their number. You take a photo, build your membership card, and share it: the wait turns into belonging.

The stadium reveal

A mask slides away as you scroll and uncovers the stadium, like raising a curtain. The construction isn't just shown: it's revealed.

The jersey and the schedule

The jersey moves with the scroll, and the team's schedule lives on the home page with the next match highlighted. Soccer today, not in two years.

Why it matters

Emotion, all the time

A club that's just being born doesn't have decades of history to tell. It has something better: the privilege of inviting people in from the very start. The site is designed for exactly that.

  • Real progress, not promises. Every visit shows the project moving forward: construction, founders, countdown.
  • The wait becomes belonging. Fans don't watch from the outside: they have a name, a number, and a membership card.
  • There's always a reason to come back. The site changes with every milestone, and every change is news.
