EdTech Hub

The LMS existed for administrators to track compliance. We rebuilt it so learners would actually want to use it.

Context

An online education startup had built a learning management system that successfully tracked course completions for B2B clients — but learner satisfaction scores were consistently low. Users described the experience as "like a government portal." Course completion rates sat at 38%.

The underlying course content was high quality. The problem was entirely experiential — users couldn't find what they needed, couldn't see their progress, and had no sense of momentum. The platform had been built for the HR teams buying it, not the employees using it.

We ran a 2-week Discovery Sprint to map friction points, then stayed on for 10 weeks of design and front-end implementation.

Our Solution

We restructured the information architecture around learning journeys, not course catalogs. The homepage was rebuilt as a personalized feed showing progress, upcoming live sessions, and peer activity. A "skill track" system replaced linear course lists, letting learners see how individual lessons connected to the competencies they were building — turning isolated content into a coherent path forward.
