NeuroCloud

Cloud cost overruns aren't an engineering failure. They're a visibility failure. We fixed the visibility.

Context

A cloud management startup had a powerful predictive analytics engine that could detect infrastructure anomalies and cost overruns before they materialized — but the dashboard engineers used to interact with it was overwhelming. Over 200 configurable widgets, no defaults, no opinionated views. Engineers spent more time configuring the dashboard than acting on its insights.

Dashboard adoption across their enterprise customer base sat at 23%. Most teams had reverted to manual Slack alerts and weekly cost reviews — exactly the behavior the product was designed to eliminate.

We ran a full product redesign with embedded front-end engineering, replacing the blank-canvas approach with an opinionated, role-based interface.

Our Solution

We replaced the blank-canvas with opinionated default views organized around the three things DevOps teams care about: availability, cost, and performance. Anomaly alerts were redesigned as an action-oriented feed — each alert linked directly to the remediation workflow. A "situation room" mode gave on-call engineers a stripped-down single-screen view optimized for incident response, reducing the cognitive load at exactly the moment it mattered most.
