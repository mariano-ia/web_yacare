FinTech App

Banking for Latin America wasn't a technology problem. It was a design problem.

Context

A fintech startup targeting the 200M+ unbanked and underbanked population in Latin America needed a mobile product capable of handling cross-border payments, local cash networks, and multiple currencies — without overwhelming users who had never owned a bank account before.

The regulatory landscape varied country-by-country, and the onboarding flow had to handle document types from Argentina, Mexico, and Brazil simultaneously. The existing prototype converted at 18% — less than 1 in 5 users who started onboarding completed it.

Our engagement started with a discovery sprint to map friction points, then moved into a full redesign of the onboarding flow and core transaction experience.

Our Solution

We designed a progressive onboarding system that collected only the minimum viable identity data per country, deferring advanced verification to contextual in-app prompts. The home screen was rebuilt around the three actions users performed most: send, receive, and convert. Local payment rails — Pix, SPEI, Transferencias — were abstracted behind a single unified interface, removing the cognitive load of knowing which rail to use.
