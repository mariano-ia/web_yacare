The existing search forced vintage shoppers into rigid boxes — categories, sizes, brands. But vintage shopping is emotional. People browse by feeling, not by filter.

THE
CONTEXT

A secondhand fashion startup trying to break into a market dominated by Depop and Vinted.

The client had a functioning beta with around 2,000 active sellers and decent inventory, but user retention was tanking. Session lengths were under 90 seconds and fewer than 12% of visitors added items to their carts.

The assumption from the product team was that the problem was algorithmic — better recommendations would fix it. Our job was to validate (or challenge) that assumption before any engineering resources were committed.

We proposed a 6-week engagement: 2 weeks of research, 2 weeks of design, 2 weeks of prototyping and testing.

Our
Process.

From raw uncertainty to a validated, testable design in six weeks.

1

Discovery & Research

12 in-depth interviews with vintage shoppers aged 18–35. We followed participants through real shopping sessions on competing apps, noting language patterns, frustration points, and delight moments.

2

Synthesis & Insight

Affinity mapping across 140+ observations surfaced a recurring theme: users didn't think in categories — they thought in aesthetics. "70s boho", "clean prep", "dark academia." No app was speaking that language.

3

Design & Prototype

We redesigned the discovery flow around aesthetic collections ("Vibes"), introduced era-based filtering, and simplified the seller tagging system. Delivered as a high-fidelity Figma prototype with 40+ screens.

4

Usability Testing

8 moderated sessions using Maze. We tested task completion, time-to-first-save, and NPS. Key finding: 7/8 participants completed a full purchase flow without assistance — up from 3/8 on the original design.

Key Insight
"I don't know what size I want. I know I want something that feels like a road trip in 1974."

— Participant #7, 26, vintage shopper for 4 years

The Solution

Four core design changes that shifted the product from category-first to vibe-first.

Vibe Collections

The home screen now leads with curated aesthetic collections — "Dark Academia", "70s Boho", "Clean Prep" — instead of category tabs. Visual, emotional, immediate.

Era & Aesthetic Filters

New filter dimensions: Decade (60s–90s), Aesthetic, Condition Story. Size and category moved to secondary filters — available but not the entry point.

Shop the Look

Outfit boards created by curators and top sellers let users buy a full aesthetic in one session. Each item links back to its individual listing.

Smarter Seller Tagging

We redesigned the listing flow so sellers tag aesthetics and vibes, not just size and condition. Guided tags reduced listing time by 35% in testing.

THE
OUTCOME

Results measured 30 days post-launch of the redesigned discovery experience.

Session Time

Average session length grew from 87 seconds to 2 minutes 22 seconds — a 40% increase in time spent exploring the app.

+40%

Cart Abandonment

Users finding items via Vibe Collections had a 28% lower cart abandonment rate than those using the traditional category search.

−28%

Wishlist Saves

Items saved per session tripled. The wishlist became a discovery tool — users built "mood boards" of items they'd come back for.
