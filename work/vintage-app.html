Vintage App

The existing search forced vintage shoppers into rigid boxes — categories, sizes, brands. But vintage shopping is emotional. People browse by feeling, not by filter.

Context

A secondhand fashion startup trying to break into a market dominated by Depop and Vinted. The client had a functioning beta with around 2,000 active sellers and decent inventory, but user retention was tanking. Session lengths were under 90 seconds and fewer than 12% of visitors added items to their carts.

The assumption from the product team was that the problem was algorithmic — better recommendations would fix it. Our job was to validate (or challenge) that assumption before any engineering resources were committed.

We proposed a 6-week engagement: 2 weeks of research, 2 weeks of design, 2 weeks of prototyping and testing. Twelve in-depth user interviews later, the real problem became clear: the interface spoke the language of inventory management, not the language of desire.

Our Solution

We redesigned the entire discovery experience around aesthetic identity — shifting from category-first to vibe-first. Instead of asking users "what are you looking for?", the app started asking "how do you want to feel?"
